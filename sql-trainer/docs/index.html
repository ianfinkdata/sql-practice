Understanding first, typing second

Most people don't get stuck on the syntax of SQL — they get stuck on the idea. So SQL Trainer explains every concept in plain English first, and only reaches for code when you're actually ready to write it. A patient mentor named Sage guides the way, meeting you exactly at your level.

"Meet the learner where they are, keep it simple, and only reach for code when the moment actually calls for it."

The Dialect Decoder

SQL is a family of closely related languages, not one language — like British vs American English. The Decoder gives you the exact phrasing for your database and the equivalent in the others, so a tutorial written for a different system never stops you cold.

🟥 Oracle SQL

Enterprise OLTP workhorse. Deep PL/SQL, sequences, MERGE, DUAL.

🧱 Databricks SQL

Lakehouse / Spark SQL for analytics. Delta, QUALIFY, semi-structured.

🐬 MySQL

The popular open-source OLTP database. AUTO_INCREMENT, backticks.

🐘 PostgreSQL

Standards-leaning and feature-rich. :: casts, jsonb, ON CONFLICT.

A taste of the translation matrix:

TaskOracleDatabricksMySQLPostgres
Limit to 10 rows FETCH FIRST 10 ROWS ONLY LIMIT 10 LIMIT 10 LIMIT 10
Null fallback NVL(x, 0) coalesce(x, 0) IFNULL(x, 0) COALESCE(x, 0)
Concatenate a || b concat(a, b) CONCAT(a, b) a || b
Truncate to month TRUNC(d, 'MM') date_trunc('month', d) DATE_FORMAT(d,'%Y-%m-01') date_trunc('month', d)
Upsert MERGE MERGE INTO ON DUPLICATE KEY UPDATE ON CONFLICT DO UPDATE

Get started

Three ways in, depending on how you like to learn.

Learning on your own

Open Tier 0 — Orientation and work tier by tier. Keep the Dialect Decoder open in another tab for your database. Practice prompts wait at the end of every module.

Learning with an AI assistant

Point your assistant at persona_instructions.md. It adopts Sage, a mentor who keeps things simple and only shows code when you need it. Then just say where you are — "I've never written SQL" or "I know joins but not window functions."
